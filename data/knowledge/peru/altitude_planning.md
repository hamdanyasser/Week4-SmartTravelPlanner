# Peru Altitude and Planning Notes

Peru should not be planned like a simple warm-weather escape. Cusco and the
Sacred Valley sit at altitude, so the first days should be slower, with buffer
time before demanding hikes. Travelers who rush straight into trekking risk
feeling unwell.

Budget can be moderate on the ground, but guided treks, Machu Picchu logistics,
and long-haul flights raise the total trip cost. July weather is favorable for
the Andes, but crowding is significant around the famous sites.

Peru is a strong answer when culture and hiking outrank beach warmth. It is a
weaker answer for users who want low-friction planning or somewhere close and
quiet.
