# Georgia Planning and Budget Notes

Georgia can stretch a budget well once the traveler is in the country. Family
guesthouses, shared transfers, and local restaurants keep daily costs moderate.
The bigger uncertainty is international airfare and the time cost of reaching
mountain regions.

For two weeks, a focused route works better than trying to see everything:
Tbilisi, Kazbegi, Svaneti, and a wine-region stop is already a full itinerary.
Travelers should expect some long drives and variable road conditions.

Georgia is a good RAG candidate when the query mentions hiking, culture,
budget awareness, and wanting somewhere less predictable than the standard
Mediterranean summer loop.
